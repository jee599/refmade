Our Mission

"We don't just build wells. We restore entire river ecosystems — from source to village — so communities can sustain clean water for generations, not just seasons."

Riverlight works with local engineers, environmental scientists, and village councils to design water systems that fit the land and the people. Every project takes 8-14 months. We stay involved for 5 years after completion to ensure the system thrives.

Impact Stories

The people behind the numbers

Mekong Delta, Vietnam

The village that stopped boiling

In Tan Hiep, families spent 3 hours daily boiling river water. After our filtration system, that time went to school, farming, and rest. Waterborne illness dropped 89% in year one.

Shan State, Myanmar

A school that finally has running water

The school in Hsipaw served 340 students with two buckets from a contaminated creek. Now a gravity-fed system provides clean water to classrooms, kitchens, and latrines.

Siem Reap, Cambodia

When the river came back

Industrial runoff had killed the Srok Sar tributary. After 14 months of wetland restoration, fish returned. Then the fishing families returned. Then the market reopened.

How It Works

From donation to clean water

1

Assessment

Our team spends 2-4 weeks with the community, mapping water sources, testing contamination levels, and understanding daily usage patterns. Nothing starts without local partnership.

2

Design & Build

Engineers design a system specific to the terrain — gravity-fed, solar-powered, or filtration-based. Local workers are hired and trained. Construction takes 4-8 months depending on complexity.

3

Monitor & Maintain

We install IoT water quality sensors and train a local maintenance team. Quarterly check-ins continue for 5 years. If something breaks, we fix it — that's the commitment.
